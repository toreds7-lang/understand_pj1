# Self-Attention
_Type: mechanism | Papers: attentionisallyouneed_

## Summary
Self-attention is a mechanism that identifies and assigns varying degrees of importance to different parts of a sequence of data when processing neural networks. It is especially significant in natural language processing and other sequence-based tasks, as it enables models to weigh different words differently within a sentence to capture context more effectively.

## Where It Appears
- **Attention is All You Need**: The paper introduces self-attention as a key component of the Transformer architecture, enabling it to model input sequences by considering all elements simultaneously, effectively capturing dependencies between distant sequence elements.

## Related Concepts
- [[transformer]]: The Transformer architecture utilizes self-attention as a core component, replacing the need for recurrence or convolution to model sequence data.
- [[uszkoreit_jakob]]: Jakob Uszkoreit, one of the co-authors of the foundational Transformer paper, helped pioneer the move from traditional RNN architectures to self-attention mechanisms.
