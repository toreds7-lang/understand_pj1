## Summary
Centralized Control refers to a system management approach where decisions and instructions are issued from a single decision-maker or a central command unit. This mechanism is often crucial for ensuring coherent strategy execution and avoiding conflicts between different subsystems. Centralized Control is significant in various multi-agent systems, where orchestrating the behavior of individual agents from a central point can lead to more efficient and predictable outcomes.
