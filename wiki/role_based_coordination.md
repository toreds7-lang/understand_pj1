## Summary
Role-Based Coordination is a mechanism utilized within multi-agent systems where agents are assigned specific roles that dictate their behavior and interactions within the system. This approach simplifies coordination by structuring the interactions based on predefined roles, allowing for efficient task allocation and collaboration among agents. It is significant because it facilitates systematic coordination in complex systems, enhancing the scalability and effectiveness of agent interactions.
